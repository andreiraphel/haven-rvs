# HAVEN-RVS Project Explanations

## 1. Process on the selection of ML technique
During the development of the predictive model, several baseline machine learning techniques were evaluated to determine which could best mirror the complex manual structural engineering computations:
*   **Linear Regression:** Was tested but resulted in a lower R² score (~0.82). It failed to capture the non-linear, "multiplicative" nature of the risk formula (Hazard × Exposure × Vulnerability).
*   **Random Forest:** Performed significantly better (R² ~0.96) and was highly accurate, but it still lacked the extreme mathematical precision needed for structural safety scoring.
*   **XGBoost (eXtreme Gradient Boosting):** Was ultimately selected. Its gradient boosting algorithm excels at capturing complex interactions between variables, providing the extreme precision necessary to replicate the exact manual engineering outputs.

## 2. Explanation on the result of ML technique selection
The selection of XGBoost resulted in a highly robust "Dual-Engine" machine learning system consisting of two specialized models:
*   **Index Regressor (XGBRegressor):** Predicts the exact continuous Risk Index (on a 0-10 scale). It achieved an unprecedented R² Score of 0.9998, meaning its predictions are virtually identical to manual spreadsheet logic, with a Mean Absolute Error of less than 0.005.
*   **Category Classifier (XGBClassifier):** Directly predicts the categorical Risk Level (Low, Moderate, High). Trained on perfectly balanced synthetic data, it achieves greater than 99.9% accuracy across all test samples.

## 3. Explanation on why Google Gemini AI is used on the assessment summary
Google Gemini AI (specifically the `gemini-2.5-flash` model) is integrated to bridge the gap between raw statistical data and actionable engineering advice. 
*   **Narrative Translation:** It consumes the raw numerical data (Risk Index, multi-select Hazard features, Vulnerability flags) and generates a human-readable, professional report.
*   **Engineering Persona:** It is prompted to act as a "Lead Structural Forensic Engineer and Heritage Conservation Specialist," ensuring the tone is forensic, conservative, and directly references actual building codes (like NSCP 2015 and RA 10066).
*   **Actionable Output:** It automatically structures a 5-sentence technical summary and a 6-step Prioritized Course of Action (prioritizing life safety, non-destructive testing, retrofitting, etc.), making the raw data immediately useful for decision-makers.

## 4. Explanation on each TechStack
*   **Frontend (Next.js & React):** Provides a fast, server-rendered web application framework ensuring a smooth, dynamic user interface with secure routing.
*   **Styling (Tailwind CSS):** A utility-first CSS framework used to rapidly build a modern, responsive, and consistent user interface without writing custom CSS files.
*   **Backend & Database (Supabase / PostgreSQL):** Serves as the primary database for storing building profiles, hazard indicators, and user data. It provides secure, robust data management and authentication.
*   **Machine Learning (Python & XGBoost):** Python is the backbone of the ML pipeline, utilizing the XGBoost library for training the highly accurate regression and classification models based on structural risk factors.
*   **Generative AI (Google Gemini API):** Handles the natural language processing to generate automated, code-compliant engineering narratives.
*   **Export Utilities (docx, exceljs, jspdf):** Client-side libraries utilized to export the complex web-based assessment results into universally accessible formats (Word, Excel, PDF) for local archival.
*   **Mapping (Google Maps API):** Integrates interactive geographical maps to visualize the distribution of building risks directly on the dashboard.

## 5. Basis on Numerical Rating (Low, Moderate, High)
The numerical rating is derived from a strict mathematical formulation that evaluates three primary indicators: Hazard, Exposure, and Vulnerability.
*   **Calculation Method:** Each indicator receives a weighted rating score (maximum of 3.0). The raw Risk Rating is calculated as the multiplicative product of the three: `Hazard Rating × Exposure Rating × Vulnerability Rating`. 
*   **Normalization:** Since the maximum possible raw score is 27 (3 × 3 × 3), the value is mathematically normalized to a standard 0 to 10 scale using the formula: `(Risk Rating / 27) * 10`.
*   **Categorical Thresholds:** This resulting 0-10 Risk Index directly maps to the categorical Risk Level based on specific engineering thresholds:
    *   **LOW RISK:** 0.00 to 1.9
    *   **MODERATE RISK:** 2.0 to 6.1
    *   **HIGH RISK:** 6.2 to 10.00
